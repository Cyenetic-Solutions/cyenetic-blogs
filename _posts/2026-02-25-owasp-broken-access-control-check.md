## What Does "Broken Access Control" Actually Mean for Your Business?

Think of access control like the locks and keys in your office building:

- **Authentication** = Checking someone's ID at the door (logging in with a username and password).
- **Authorization / Access Control** = Deciding which floors, rooms, or files that person is actually allowed into once they're inside.

**Broken Access Control** happens when the building lets someone wander into the CEO's office, open the safe, or walk into a restricted server room — even though they only had clearance for the lobby.

In your web app or API, this looks like:

- A normal user tweaks a link and views someone else's private data (e.g., changing `/my-account/123` to `/my-account/456`).
- An attacker tricks the system into acting as an administrator.
- Someone bypasses restrictions to delete records, change prices, or access internal tools.

The **OWASP Top 10:2025** confirms this remains the top risk — it was recorded **1,839,701 times** between 2021 and 2024. For businesses handling customer data, payments, health records, or proprietary information, this isn't a "tech problem." It's a direct threat to your revenue, reputation, and legal standing.
